Irish Soda Bread

Irish Soda Bread cut into slices.

This is one of my favorite bread recipies because it doesn't require yeast to rise.

Ingredients

  • ½ cup white sugar
  • 4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon baking soda
  • ¾ teaspoon salt
  • 3 cups raisins
  • 1 tablespoon caraway seeds
  • 2 eggs, lightly beaten
  • 1¼ cups buttermilk
  • 1 cup sour cream

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 350F (175C). Grease a 9-inch round cast iron skillet or a 9-inch round baking or cake pan.
  2. In a mixing bowl, combine flour (reserving 1 tablespoon), sugar, baking powder, baking soda, salt, raisins, and caraway seeds. In a small bowl, blend eggs, buttermilk and sour cream. Stir the liquid mixture into flour mixture just until flour is moistened.
  3. Knead dough in bowl about 10 to 12 strokes. Dough will be sticky. Place the dough in the prepared skillet or pan and pat down. Cut a 4x¾-inch deep slit in the top of the bread. Dust with reserved flour.
  4. Bake in the preheated oven for 65 to 75 minutes. Let cool and turn bread onto a wire rack.
